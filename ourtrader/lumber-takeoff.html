Our Estimation Process

The expert estimators at our firm have the complete knowledge of construction techniques, wall alignments, lumber quality, temporary bracing, and reinforcement, and easily pinpoint the structural issues, product suitability, and substitutes.

Our estimation process starts with a review of drawing plans, material specifications, usages, field requirements, delivery sequences, etc. provided by the client to define the scope of work. This is followed by the on-screen takeoff using the latest software like Planswift, Bluebeam, Trimble, etc. to import the plans and accurately measure the dimensions.

All the lumber quantities required for framing are exported to EXCEL spreadsheets that are well referenced with color-coded plans. Our takeoff includes the breakdown of the following quantities but is not limited to:

  • Wooden posts, with different types and sizes
  • Structural lumber components including framing panels, sill plates, wall studs, corner studs, ladder studs, etc.
  • Insulation gaps and sheathings
  • Rough carpentry and timber accessories
  • Floor decking & roof decking
  • Window/door casings including headers, beams, cripples, and trimmers
  • Joists, rafters, & trusses

We perform a precise lumber cut list to ensure the usage of maximum wooden posts to avoid wastage by cutting lengths. It also helps framers to properly utilize each and every piece of timber.

We estimate the cost of your project based on your requirements. This means that we can either send you the quoted material lists to forward to your suppliers or we can utilize our construction cost database and RSMeans for up to date and location-based pricing. And for labor costs, we can work with your labor rates as well as union or non-union and prevailing wages as per your demand.

Our estimates include all the direct and indirect costs like lumber costs, transportation costs, insurances, safety costs, cleanups, and other miscellaneous costs.
